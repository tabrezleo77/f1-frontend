Internal Combustion Engine (ICE)

The 1.6L V6 turbo ICE prioritizes efficiency and longevity over peak RPM. Engines are detuned to survive multiple race weekends under extreme stress.

Turbocharger

Operating beyond 120,000 rpm, the turbocharger faces extreme thermal shock and relies on precise electronic control and lubrication.

MGU-K

The MGU-K recovers braking energy and redeploys up to 120 kW to the crankshaft, demanding flawless cooling and torque control.

MGU-H

Mounted on the turbo shaft, the MGU-H controls turbo speed and energy harvesting under extreme heat, vibration, and rotational stress.

Energy Store

A high-density lithium-ion battery handling rapid charge and discharge cycles. Thermal management and cell balance define its reliability.

Control Electronics (CE)

Control electronics manage energy flow across the power unit. Software reliability is as critical as hardware durability.

Fuel & Lubrication System

High-pressure fuel injection and dry-sump lubrication demand absolute precision. Any pressure loss can destroy the engine within seconds.

Cooling Systems

Multiple cooling loops manage engine, oil, ERS, and charge air temperatures. Cooling limitations often dictate race strategy.

Gearbox

The eight-speed sequential gearbox is a stressed structural component. Smooth torque delivery is essential for drivetrain reliability.
